Welcome!



My name is Daniel and I'm a PhD student in the department of economics and finance at Durham University. The guiding drive behind my academic work is to better understand weather-related risks and the economics of adaptation in a changing climate. My PhD research is supervised by Riccardo Scarpa and Ashar Aftab in collaboration with Sim Reaney and focuses on designing spatially targeted incentive schemes for natural flood risk management.

I hold an MA in economics from the Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences, where I was also a research assistant in the economics department. During my studies I spent a semester in the department of environmental science at ETH Zurich, and retain a keen interest in environmental GIS and climate.
