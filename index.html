Welcome!

My name is Daniel and I'm a PhD student in the department of economics and finance at Durham University Business School. My research aims to better understand weather-related risks and the economics of adaptation in a changing climate. My PhD research is supervised by Riccardo Scarpa and Ashar Aftab in collaboration with the Institute of Hazard, Risk and Resilience, Durham University, and focuses on designing spatially targeted incentive schemes for natural flood risk management. I am especially interested in how beliefs influence decision-making on environmental risks, and in the role networks - social, spatial and cultural - in forming those beliefs.

I hold an MA in economics from the Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences, where I was also a research assistant in the economics department. During my studies I spent a semester in the department of environmental science at ETH Zurich, and retain a keen interest in environmental GIS and climate.
